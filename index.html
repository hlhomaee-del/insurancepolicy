Health Insurance Types

Commercial health plan
Health coverage issued by a private insurer or private entity, often employer-sponsored or individual market. It is not a government program simply by default.
Private health plan
Nongovernment health coverage obtained through a private source, including employer-sponsored or individually purchased plans. In many CBCS contexts it overlaps with commercial health insurance.
Indemnity insurance
A type of plan that traditionally pays based on the policy’s terms or a set benefit structure rather than tightly managed network design. Students should distinguish it from managed care style plans.
Supplemental health plans
Additional coverage that pays alongside major medical coverage, often limited or cash-benefit style, rather than replacing primary health insurance.

Disability & Income Protection

🧾

Disability insurance

Broadly refers to coverage connected to disability-related financial protection. Students must ask whether the policy is about medical expenses or wage replacement.

💸

Disability income plan

Pays income replacement when a person cannot work due to disability. This is not the same thing as major medical payment for treatment.

📆

Base earnings period

A prior earnings period used to determine eligibility or benefit amounts in disability-related programs. The key idea is that past wages can affect present eligibility.

CBCS trap: students often think “disability” automatically means the insurance pays the doctor bill. Many disability-income concepts are about wage replacement, not provider reimbursement.

Liability & Legal Concepts

Auto insurance policy
Coverage connected to motor vehicle incidents. Depending on the policy and state rules, it may involve liability, medical payments, no-fault/PIP, or property damage components.
Liability coverage
Coverage that responds when the insured is legally responsible for harm or damages to another person or property.
Legal lien claim
A legal claim against money or property, often against settlement proceeds, to secure repayment of a debt or reimbursable amount.
Subrogation right
A recovery right that allows an insurer or payer that already paid a claim to pursue reimbursement from the responsible third party.

Who pays → who gets reimbursed

Example flow:
Patient injured in auto accident → insurer or payer covers eligible medical expense → later there is a liability recovery or settlement → lien/subrogation may affect who gets reimbursed from those proceeds.

Key distinction

Liability coverage is a coverage type tied to legal responsibility.
Subrogation right is a recovery right after payment.
Legal lien claim is a legal claim against proceeds or property.
